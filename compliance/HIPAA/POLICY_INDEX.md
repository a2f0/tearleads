# HIPAA Policy Index

This index catalogs the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) compliance requirements for agent-based compliance demonstration.

- **Governing Body**: U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS)
- **Enforcement**: Office for Civil Rights (OCR)

## Core Rules

### 1. Privacy Rule (45 CFR Part 164, Subpart E)

Establishes national standards for the protection of individually identifiable health information.

#### Policy Areas

- **Permitted Uses and Disclosures** (164.502, 164.506): Defines when PHI may be used/disclosed without authorization
- **Minimum Necessary Standard** (164.502(b)): Limits PHI access to minimum necessary for intended purpose
- **Individual Rights** (164.524-164.528): Rights to access, amend, and receive accounting of disclosures
- **Notice of Privacy Practices** (164.520): Required notice describing PHI uses and individual rights
- **Authorization Requirements** (164.508): When written authorization is required for PHI use
- **De-identification Standards** (164.514): Methods for de-identifying PHI
- **Business Associate Agreements** (164.504): Required contracts with third parties handling PHI

### 2. Security Rule (45 CFR Part 164, Subpart C)

Establishes standards for protecting electronic protected health information (ePHI).

#### 2.1 Administrative Safeguards (164.308)

##### Security Management Process

- Risk Analysis (Required)
- Risk Management (Required)
- Sanction Policy (Required)
- Information System Activity Review (Required)

##### Assigned Security Responsibility

- Designate Security Official (Required)

##### Workforce Security

- Authorization/Supervision (Addressable)
- Workforce Clearance Procedure (Addressable)
- Termination Procedures (Addressable)

##### Information Access Management

- Access Authorization (Addressable)
- Access Establishment/Modification (Addressable)
- Isolating Healthcare Clearinghouse Functions (Required)

##### Security Awareness and Training

- Security Reminders (Addressable)
- Protection from Malicious Software (Addressable)
- Log-in Monitoring (Addressable)
- Password Management (Addressable)

##### Security Incident Procedures

- Response and Reporting (Required)

##### Contingency Plan

- Data Backup Plan (Required)
- Disaster Recovery Plan (Required)
- Emergency Mode Operation Plan (Required)
- Testing and Revision Procedures (Addressable)
- Applications and Data Criticality Analysis (Addressable)

##### Evaluation

- Periodic Technical/Non-Technical Evaluation (Required)

##### Business Associate Contracts

- Written Contract or Arrangement (Required)

#### 2.2 Physical Safeguards (164.310)

##### Facility Access Controls

- Contingency Operations (Addressable)
- Facility Security Plan (Addressable)
- Access Control and Validation Procedures (Addressable)
- Maintenance Records (Addressable)

##### Workstation Use

- Policies for Proper Workstation Use (Required)

##### Workstation Security

- Physical Safeguards for Workstations (Required)

##### Device and Media Controls

- Disposal (Required)
- Media Re-use (Required)
- Accountability (Addressable)
- Data Backup and Storage (Addressable)

#### 2.3 Technical Safeguards (164.312)

##### Access Control

- Unique User Identification (Required)
- Emergency Access Procedure (Required)
- Automatic Logoff (Addressable)
- Encryption and Decryption (Addressable)

##### Audit Controls

- Hardware/Software/Procedural Mechanisms (Required)

##### Integrity

- Mechanism to Authenticate ePHI (Addressable)

##### Person or Entity Authentication

- Verify Identity of Persons/Entities (Required)

##### Transmission Security

- Integrity Controls (Addressable)
- Encryption (Addressable)

#### 2.4 Organizational Requirements (164.314)

- Business Associate Contracts: Satisfactory assurances from business associates
- Group Health Plan Requirements: Plan documents and safeguards

#### 2.5 Policies and Procedures (164.316)

- Policies and Procedures: Implement reasonable and appropriate policies
- Documentation: Maintain written records; retain for 6 years

### 3. Breach Notification Rule (45 CFR Part 164, Subpart D)

Requires notification following a breach of unsecured PHI.

#### Individual Notification

- Notify affected individuals in writing
- Timeline: Within 60 days of discovery

#### Media Notification

- Notify prominent media outlets when 500+ affected in state
- Timeline: Within 60 days of discovery

#### HHS Notification (Major Breach)

- Report breaches affecting 500+ individuals
- Timeline: Within 60 days of discovery

#### HHS Notification (Minor Breach)

- Report breaches affecting fewer than 500 individuals
- Timeline: Annually within 60 days of year end

#### Business Associate Notification

- BA must notify covered entity of breach
- Timeline: Within 60 days of discovery

## Covered Entities

- **Health Plans**: Health insurance companies, HMOs, employer-sponsored plans
- **Healthcare Clearinghouses**: Entities processing healthcare transactions
- **Healthcare Providers**: Providers conducting electronic transactions
- **Business Associates**: Third parties handling PHI on behalf of covered entities

---

## Key Definitions

- **PHI**: Protected Health Information - individually identifiable health information
- **ePHI**: Electronic Protected Health Information
- **Covered Entity**: Health plan, healthcare clearinghouse, or healthcare provider
- **Business Associate**: Person/organization performing functions involving PHI
- **Breach**: Unauthorized acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of PHI
- **Minimum Necessary**: Limiting PHI to minimum needed for intended purpose

---

## Enforcement and Penalties

- **Unknowing**: $100 - $50,000 per violation (Annual max: $25,000)
- **Reasonable Cause**: $1,000 - $50,000 per violation (Annual max: $100,000)
- **Willful Neglect (Corrected)**: $10,000 - $50,000 per violation (Annual max: $250,000)
- **Willful Neglect (Not Corrected)**: $50,000 per violation (Annual max: $1,500,000)

## 2024-2025 Updates

### Proposed Security Rule Modifications (December 2024 NPRM)

- Removal of "required" vs "addressable" distinction
- All implementation specifications become required (with limited exceptions)
- Enhanced cybersecurity requirements for ePHI protection

### Privacy Rule Update (April 2024)

- HIPAA Privacy Rule to Support Reproductive Health Care Privacy
- Effective June 25, 2024; Compliance date December 23, 2024
