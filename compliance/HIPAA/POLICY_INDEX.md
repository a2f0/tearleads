# HIPAA Policy Index

This index catalogs the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) compliance requirements for agent-based compliance demonstration.

## Regulatory Authority

- **Governing Body**: U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS)
- **Enforcement**: Office for Civil Rights (OCR)
- **Regulatory Citation**: 45 CFR Part 160 and Part 164

## Core Rules

### 1. Privacy Rule (45 CFR Part 164, Subpart E)

Establishes national standards for the protection of individually identifiable health information.

| Policy Area | Description | Citation |
|-------------|-------------|----------|
| Permitted Uses and Disclosures | Defines when PHI may be used/disclosed without authorization | 164.502, 164.506 |
| Minimum Necessary Standard | Limits PHI access to minimum necessary for intended purpose | 164.502(b) |
| Individual Rights | Rights to access, amend, and receive accounting of disclosures | 164.524-164.528 |
| Notice of Privacy Practices | Required notice describing PHI uses and individual rights | 164.520 |
| Authorization Requirements | When written authorization is required for PHI use | 164.508 |
| De-identification Standards | Methods for de-identifying PHI | 164.514 |
| Business Associate Agreements | Required contracts with third parties handling PHI | 164.504 |

### 3. Breach Notification Rule (45 CFR Part 164, Subpart D)

Requires notification following a breach of unsecured PHI.

| Requirement | Description | Timeline |
|-------------|-------------|----------|
| Individual Notification | Notify affected individuals in writing | Within 60 days of discovery |
| Media Notification | Notify prominent media outlets (500+ affected in state) | Within 60 days of discovery |
| HHS Notification (Major) | Report breaches affecting 500+ individuals | Within 60 days of discovery |
| HHS Notification (Minor) | Report breaches affecting <500 individuals | Annually within 60 days of year end |
| Business Associate Notification | BA must notify covered entity of breach | Within 60 days of discovery |

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| PHI | Protected Health Information - individually identifiable health information |
| ePHI | Electronic Protected Health Information |
| Covered Entity | Health plan, healthcare clearinghouse, or healthcare provider |
| Business Associate | Person/organization performing functions involving PHI |
| Breach | Unauthorized acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of PHI |
| Minimum Necessary | Limiting PHI to minimum needed for intended purpose |

## Enforcement and Penalties

| Violation Category | Penalty Range (per violation) | Annual Maximum |
|--------------------|-------------------------------|----------------|
| Unknowing | $100 - $50,000 | $25,000 |
| Reasonable Cause | $1,000 - $50,000 | $100,000 |
| Willful Neglect (Corrected) | $10,000 - $50,000 | $250,000 |
| Willful Neglect (Not Corrected) | $50,000 | $1,500,000 |

## 2024-2025 Updates

### Proposed Security Rule Modifications (December 2024 NPRM)
- Removal of "required" vs "addressable" distinction
- All implementation specifications become required (with limited exceptions)
- Enhanced cybersecurity requirements for ePHI protection

### Privacy Rule Update (April 2024)
- HIPAA Privacy Rule to Support Reproductive Health Care Privacy
- Effective June 25, 2024; Compliance date December 23, 2024
